BioMAP inference

Distribution of CLOGP property

Distribution of MW property

Distribution of TPSA property

In this section we present the results of the experiment aimed at inferring BioMAP cytotox and diversity biomarkers independently from L1000 dataset using drug perturbation response data. We utilised 280 different model architectures that vary by feature selection method (S_523, f_score, fisher_score, reliefF), number of input features (200, 300, 500, 978) and algorithm (ElasticNet, Linear regression, KNN, RF, ExtraTrees, SVR, MLP). As an input for a models we utilised robust z-scores for landmark genes from L1000 dataset and as an output the ratio ‘Log-Ratio/Confidence interval’ for BioMAP cytotox and diversity data. Robust z-scores were averaged for perturbation replicates. Input L1000 samples and output BioMAP samples were matched by drug and concentration. Also for L1000 dataset we experimented with addition of provided molecular properties (# of aromatic rings, clogp, molecular weight, TPSA) as extra features.


Since the number of common with L1000 drugs profiled in cytotox and diversity BioMAP sets is different we used two separate output sets: cytotox and diversity. We also used 4 different training set designs to stratify the L1000 set:


  1. Data set separated by time point (6 and 24 hours) with robust z-scores for landmark genes (Landmarks_by_time).
  2. Data set separated by time point (6 and 24 hours) with robust z-scores for landmark genes and values for 4 additional molecular properties (Landmarks_and_molprop_by_time).
  3. Aggregated data set with robust z-scores for landmark genes (Landmarks).
  4. Aggregated data set with robust z-scores for landmark genes and values for 4 additional molecular properties (Landmarks_and_molprop).

For each of the training sets feature selection methods were applied to L1000 landmark genes followed by building ML and DL models to predict the BioMAP feature. Each BioMAP biomarker was inferred independently. For each ML and DL model optimization of its hyperparameters was applied using grid search. Each model underwent 10-fold cross-validation and the resulting performance was averaged across folds.


In this report we present the tables with performance (mean R-squared across all biomarkers in either diversity or cytotox sets) of the models for each training set design. After each performance table we also put in separate tabs the R-squared metrics for each biomarker of top model for cytotox and top model for diversity inference. Due to curse of dimensionality and relatively small number of matching samples DNNs lost to a more conventional methods like ElasticNet and tree-based methods.

Cardiotoxicity


HMOX1 (Heme Oxygenase 1) (upregulated)  is  also  called HO-1 and its expression was found to be increased in cytoplasmic extracts in a dose-dependant manner in a study about Rosiglitazone inducing cardiotoxicity by accelerated apoptosis (Mishra et al. 2014)


BMP4 (Bone Morphogenetic Protein 4) (upregulated)  BMPR2 is known to be critical in cardiogenesis; knockdown results in embryonic lethality via abnormal homeobox expression and heart formation (Shi et al. 2000), particularly involving positioning of the outflow tracts, remodeling of the arterioventricular cushion, and development of the valve leaflets (Beppu et al. 2009)


GADD45A (Growth Arrest And DNA Damage Inducible Alpha) (upregulated) It was shown to  be a differentially expressed gene related to  various pathways affected upon doxorubicin exposure leading to doxorubicin-induced toxicity in human cardiomyocytes (Holmgren et al. 2015)


ANKRD1 (Ankyrin Repeat Domain 1) (upregulated)  Cardiac ankyrin repeat protein (CARP, ANKRD1) is a transcriptional regulatory protein that is extremely susceptible to doxorubicin. CARP expression is regulated in part by GATA4 (Chen et al. 2012).


MPO (Myeloperoxidase) (upregulated)   MPO was identified as one of two predictors of cardiotoxicity in breast cancer patients treated with ACs and Herceptin, from a panel of potential biomarkers including CRP, NT-proBNP, growth differentiation factor (GDF)-15, placenta growth factor (PlGF), soluble fms-like tyrosine kinase receptor (sFlt)-1, and galectin (gal)-3 (Ky et al. 2014)


MDH2 (Malate Dehydrogenase 2) (upregulated) mitochondrial malate dehydrogenase (MDH2) was recently discussed  as a novel target for reducing DOX cardiotoxicity (Liu et al. 2014)  It was demonstrated that treatment with structurally diverse inhibitors of MDH2 (i.e., mebendazole, thyroxine, iodine, malate)  prevented DOX cardiotoxicity (Xi 2016).


TNNT1 (Troponin T1, Slow Skeletal Type) (upregulated) it is a specific biomarkers of drug-induced skeletal muscle injury (Campion et al. 2013)


CDC42EP3 (CDC42 Effector Protein 3) (upregulated) This gene was found differentially expressed in a study about comparative phenotypic assessment of cardiac pathology (Auerbach et al. 2010).


AKAP13 (A-Kinase Anchoring Protein 13) (downregulated) Study showed that AKAP13-Mediated PKD1 signaling regulates the transcriptional response to cardiac hypertrophy (Johnson et al. 2015)


IL3RA (Interleukin 3 Receptor Subunit Alpha) (downregulated) Its role was discussed in a study about genetic contributions to cardiac pathology induced by beta-adrenergic stimulation (Rau et al. 2015)



Mitotoxicity


SPARC (Secreted Protein Acidic And Cysteine Rich) (upregulated)  overexpression of SPARC suppresses radiation-induced HSP27 expression in neuroblastoma cells and the combination of SPARC and radiation induces the expression of protein 21, but suppresses neuroblastoma tumor density. SPARC also induces mitochondrial Δψ collapse in SK-N-BE and NB1691 neuroblastoma cells (Tanpure et al. 2017) (Pinto et al. 2015)



EREG (Epiregulin) (downregulated) epiregulin significantly increased the proportion of J aggregates (from JC-1) in oocyte mitochondria and this coupled with FAD and REDOX measures indicates greater mitochondrial activity (Richani et al. 2014) (Campos-Chillon et al. 2015).  


MMP3 (Matrix Metallopeptidase 3) (downregulated) Homocysteine stimulate intracellular ROS production and mitochondria dysfunction, then decrease the cell viability and bone formation activity, the mechanism may depend on increasing MMPs, ROS production, and also dysfunction of mitochondria. results showed that an endopeptidase, matrix metalloproteinase-3 (MMP3), was induced and activated in dopaminergic (DA) cells upon stress conditions(Choi et al. 2011) .



PAK2 (P21 (RAC1) Activated Kinase 2) (downregulated) Paks regulate cell survival and death in response to cellular stimuli. Full-length Pak1, Pak2, Pak4 and Pak5 have been reported to have anti-apoptotic roles. Pak2 can be activated via cleavage by caspase proteases to release a constitutively active C-terminal fragment (Pak2p34). Caspase-activated Pak2p34 has been observed after cells are exposed to death stimuli such as Fas ligand, TNFα, heat shock or UV radiation (Luo and Rubinsztein 2009) (Chan, Yu, and Yang 1999)


DAD1 (Defender Against Cell Death 1) (upregulated) the mammalian defender against cell death-1 is involved in apoptosis regulation (Wnęk et al. 2016) (Carmona-Gutierrez et al. 2010).

Toxcast analysis


We collected ToxCast data for SAg and 3C systems from EPA. For SAg we found 10 common biomarkers that are present in investigated dataset, these include: CD38, CD40, CD69, CD62E/E-Selectin, CXCL8/IL-8, CCL2/MCP-1, CXCL9/MIG, PBMC Cytotoxicity, Proliferation, SRB. For 3C we found 12 common biomarkers: CD62E/E-Selectin, CXCL8/IL-8, CCL2/MCP-1, CXCL9/MIG, PBMC Cytotoxicity, Proliferation, SRB, CD54/ICAM-1, HLA-DR, CD141/Thrombomodulin, CD142/Tissue Factor, CD106/VCAM-1, CD87/uPAR. For each drug perturbation from BioMAP we correlated log-ratio/conf.envelope to downward curve-fit analysis readout from ToxCast. Results can be found in two following tables.


Input: Biomap datatable (Download file): biomarkers as rows, perturbations as columns; ToxCast tables with readouts for 3C (Download file) and SAg(Download file): biomarkers as columns, perturbations as rows.


Processing: Within each of the two systems (3C, SAg) for each compound perturbation pair (GSKvsToxCast) rows (biomarkers) from BioMAP datatable were matched to columns (biomarkers) of ToxCast table. For each compound pair (GSKvsToxCast) this resulted in two vectors of length 10 (in case of SAg system) and 12 (in case of 3C system). Pearson correlation was calculated for all possible compound perturbation pairs (GSKvsToxCast).


Output: A table (with ToxCast perturbations as rows and BioMAP perturbations as columns) populated with Pearson correlation values. As the calculations for 3C and SAg were done independently, two separate tables were generated.

Similarity to LINCS dataset


In this section we calculated the similarity of investigated perturbations to LINCS dataset. For each sample we performed the correlation analysis of its gene expression values and 1.3+ million samples from LINCS on the probe level. Then for each sample we picked top-200 LINCS samples by correlation coefficient and combined these lists in the resulting table. Within each perturbation we averaged correlation coefficients between two replicates. In LINCS dataset pert_ids with "BRD-", "TRCN-" and "BRDN-" prefixes designate small molecule, knock-down and ORF overexpression perturbations, respectively. In pert_desc column you can find perturbation name (name of the molecule or name of the gene in case of knock-downs and ORF overexpression).
