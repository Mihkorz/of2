Methods

Gene expression data provided by L’Óreal was processed with frozen RMA method [1]. Four functional groups were considered in the differential gene expression analysis: Resveratrol, Retinoic acid, Metformin hydrochloride and Capryloyl salicylic acid. In all the following parts of analysis gene expression data from keratinocytes before incubation with compounds was used as a reference and other groups were compared to it. For the gene-level analysis, we performed Student’s T-test. For the pathway level analysis each preprocessed gene expression group was independently compared to the reference using an algorithm called OncoFinder [2]. Using preprocessed gene expression data as an input allows for cross-platform dataset comparison with low error rate and provides the ability to obtain functional features of intracellular regulation using mathematical estimations. Positive and negative PAS values indicate pathway up- and down-regulation, respectively.
Geroscope™, an extension of Oncofinder [3], was used to calculate possible geroprotective activity of provided compounds and to perform comparison with other compounds.

Resveratrol and Capryloyl Salicylic acid showed higher scores in 50+ y/o skin, probably because of their anti-inflammatory activity (inflammation processes prevail in aged skin). Retinoic acid is beneficial for skin in low concentrations and short period of incubation (1 µm 24 hours).

Conclusions

  1. Low concentration (10 µM) of resveratrol showed more beneficial effects to skin (such as inhibition of mTOR via mTORC1 complex and absence of upregulation of stress related pathways) on a pathway level.
  2. High concentration (50 µM) of resveratrol led to the activation of cell stress response on a pathway level.
  3. Retinoic acid activated stress related pathways.
  4. Retinoic acid upregulated TGF-beta pathway after 24 hours of incubation, but showed strong downregulation of TGF-beta pathway after 48 hours of incubation.
  5. Metformin showed strong upregulation of WNT signaling pathway and upregulation of TGF-beta signaling pathway. These pathways are usually down-regulated in aged skin.
  6. Capryloyl salicylic acid demonstrated beneficial actions on skin (strong suppression of inflammation) only after 48 hours of incubation.
  7. Capryloyl salicylic acid downregulated TGF-beta pathway that causes the reduction of skin collagen.
  8. Metformin demonstrated anti-aging action on skin younger than 50 y/o on a pathway level.
  9. Capryloyl salicylic acid demonstrated anti-aging action on 50+ y/o skin on a pathway level and seemed to be a better anti-aging treatment than other salicylates, resveratrol and retinoic acid.
  10. Resveratrol demonstrated anti-aging action on 50+ y/o skin on a pathway level.
  11. Retinoic acid demonstrated anti-aging action on 50+ y/o skin on a pathway level.
